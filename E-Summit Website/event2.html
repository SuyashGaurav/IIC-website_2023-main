About The Event

Ideate is a pitch deck competition. Get together with your friends and think bigger and better to ideate your way to victory.
Ideate is a terrific way to have a good time while learning about entrepreneurship. The best social and innovation entrepreneurship ideas win and will have the opportunity to be pre-incubated. Be sure to participate and gain experience, have some fun and possibly win some great prizes!

Prizes

  • Prizes worth Rs.10000
  • Pre-Incubation provided by IIC
  • Mentoring sessions from our sponsor Startup Buddy
  • Certificate of Participation for all participants
  • Winners will be awarded Certificate of Excellence, and will be inducted into the IIC Hall of Fame

When

Monday to Sunday
12-18 July

More About the Event

Rules

  • Team Size: 2-3
  • Each pitch should take no longer than 5 mins and would be succeeded by 5 mins of Q&A with the judges
  • The themes of the pitch should be from the list mentioned below
  • All decisions in matters of eligibility, authenticity & marking made by the judges shall be final and binding.
  • Any attempts at malpractice will lead to disqualification.
  • All the event-specific rules and guidelines will be conveyed before the event.
  • The selected startup ideas will get a chance to incubate at IIT Dharwad where they will be provided with support regarding mentorship, technical expertise, sources of funding, access to innovation labs, etc by the Institute Innovation Council.
  • Organizers reserve the right to change, modify or postpone any aspect of the event without prior notice. In case of any dispute, the decision of organizers shall be final and binding.

Themes

  • Social Entrepreneurship
    • Social entrepreneurship is an approach by individuals, groups, start-up companies or entrepreneurs, in which they develop, fund and implement solutions to social, cultural, or environmental issues.
  • Innovation Entrepreneurship
    • Innovation and entrepreneurship are essential ingredients in building a successful commercial venture. The ways in which these two concepts fuel enterprise are something our faculty never stop exploring.

Rounds

  • The event will take place in two rounds.
  • Participants should register for the event through the registration form. The link for submissions will be shared via email and the participants will be judged based on the below-mentioned criteria. The top 10 teams will be selected for the final round.

    • Round 1 : Idea Screening
    • In this round, all the teams have to submit an executive summary of not more than 1000 words.
    • The executive summary should give an abstract of the participating team’s idea. The executive summary will be evaluated by the judges based on:
      • The innovation in your idea
      • The social impact of your idea
      • The real-world problem it mitigates
      • The market opportunity for the idea
      • The scalability of your idea

    Round 2 : Presentation Round
    • Top 10 teams that clear the 1st round would pitch their ideas before a panel of judges.
    • Each pitch would be no longer than 5 mins and would be succeeded by 5 mins of Q&A with the judges.
    • The presentation would be done on Zoom Video Call, the link for the same will be shared via email.

Schedule

  • 12th July, Monday: Registration closes at 11:59 p.m.
  • 13th July, Tuesday: Submission of the executive summary starts, link shared through email at 8 a.m.
  • 14th July, Wednesday: Submission of executive summary closes at 11:59 p.m.
  • 16th July, Friday: List of 10 Selected Teams announced at 8 a.m
  • 18th July, Sunday: Final Presentation Round from 2 p.m.-5 p.m.

F.A.Q.

  • How many members can a team have?

    A team must consist of 2 to 3 members.

  • What is a pitch deck competition?

    A pitch deck is a presentation deck that is used to pitch your idea or company to any number of audiences, generally investors. A pitch deck competition is a model version of the real-life counterpart where several teams compete. The audience of the pitch deck in such an event are the judges of the competition.

  • What kind of ideas are we supposed to present?

    The event revolves around two general themes “Social Entrepreneurship” and “Innovation Entrepreneurship”, any ideas will be accepted but they will be judged based on these two themes.

  • What will we be evaluated on?

    As mentioned above the entries will be judged based on the two themes of the event “Social Entrepreneurship” and “Innovation Entrepreneurship”.

  • How will the competition be conducted?

    The competition will have two rounds. The first round is an idea screening where the team will be expected to submit an executive summary of 1000 words, touching on the social impact, innovation, scalability, etc. Then the top 10 teams will move on to the finals, a presentation round where the teams will present their ideas in front of a panel of judges in about 5 minutes, followed by a Q&A. The final round will be a live round (in a virtual fashion) and will be open for public viewing.

  • Is there a limit to the executive summary?

    Yes, the summary must be under 1000 words.

  • Is there a limit for the pitch?

    Yes, the pitch should not cross the time limit of 5 minutes.

  • Will there be prizes?

    Yes, there will be prizes for the winners as well as participation certificates for all the participants.
